DESCRIPTION

The Biotopo Selva Húmeda Natural Reserve is a Natural Reserve affiliated to the National Network of Private Reserves of the Civil Population, which contains a habitat with natural formations and species of flora and fauna of invaluable value for science and the natural environment.

The wildlife in the Biotopo Selva Húmeda Natural Reserve has not been altered on a large scale by the devastating action of humans, mainly due to its enormous extension and the difficulties to access its territory. However, the flora and fauna is being threatened by the illegal exploitation of wood and other activities that destroy the ecosystem.

The Biotopo Selva Húmeda Natural Reserve was created under a principle of immense love and concern for the environment and with the superior purpose of providing humanity with a protected natural space that allows the conservation of a representative sample of the tropical rainforest of the coastal piedmont of the state of Nariño (Southern Colombia) and its biodiversity of flora and fauna. It was also created with the purpose of offering a natural space for scientific research and environmental education, guaranteeing the genetic renewal of the existing flora and fauna; protecting the species of flora and fauna that are threatened or in danger of extinction, and promoting adventure and scientific ecotourism.
